Network of Thrones

SAND Edition

This map is a zoomable, searchable network visualization of the Storm of Swords Network published by Dr. Andrew Beveridge at Macalaster College.

A. Beveridge and J. Shan, "Network of Thrones," Math Horizons Magazine , Vol. 23, No. 4 (2016), pp. 18-22.

The visualization leverages SAND, py2cytoscape, and python-igraph to implement the following features:

  • An edge's thickness represents its weight.
  • The color of a vertex's border indicates its community.
  • The size of a vertex corresponds to its PageRank value.
  • The size of its label corresponds to its betweenness centrality.
  • The layout is a degree-sorted circle based on group membership, with saved and reloadable manual positioning.
  • The vertex's background is a local copy of the character's profile image on http://gameofthrones.wikia.com.

But...why?

SAND is a network analytics toolkit and workflow written in Python. SAND focuses on the domain of network data in systems architecture, but the principles are useful for analysis of network data in other domains. The Network of Thrones dataset provides a basis to calibrate SAND against an existing network analysis. It acts as a case study and functional test for SAND, but is hopefully useful and entertaining in its own right.
